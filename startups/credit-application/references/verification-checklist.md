# Startup Credits Verification Checklist

Before applying for any startup credit programs, ensure you have the following documents and information readily available. Being prepared will make the application process much smoother and increase your chances of success.

## Core Company Information

- [ ] **Company Name and Website**: Your website should be professional and clearly describe what your company does.
- [ ] **Incorporation Documents**: Have your articles of incorporation or equivalent legal documents ready to prove that your company is a registered legal entity.
- [ ] **Company Address and Phone Number**: A professional business address is recommended.
- [ ] **Founder and Team Information**: Be prepared to provide details about the founding team, including names, roles, and contact information.

## Pitching and Business Plan

- [ ] **Elevator Pitch**: A concise (1-2 sentence) description of your company.
- [ ] **Company Description**: A more detailed (1-2 paragraph) overview of your business, the problem you are solving, and your solution.
- [ ] **Pitch Deck**: A professional pitch deck is often required, especially for larger credit packages. This should include information on your team, product, market, and traction.

## Funding and Financials

- [ ] **Funding Status**: Be clear about whether you are bootstrapped, pre-seed, seed, or Series A. Have the total amount of funding raised to date.
- [ ] **Proof of Funding**: If you are funded, have documentation ready to prove it. This can include:
    -   A Simple Agreement for Future Equity (SAFE) agreement.
    -   A term sheet from an institutional investor.
    -   A press release or news article announcing your funding round.
- [ ] **Revenue Information**: Some programs require you to be pre-revenue or have less than a certain amount of annual revenue.

## Technical Information

- [ ] **Cloud Provider Account ID**: If applying for cloud credits, have your account ID for that provider (e.g., your AWS Account ID).
- [ ] **Technical Architecture Overview**: Be prepared to briefly describe your tech stack and how you plan to use the credits.

## Optional but Recommended

- [ ] **Crunchbase or PitchBook Profile**: Having a profile on these platforms can add legitimacy to your application.
- [ ] **LinkedIn Company Page**: A professional and up-to-date LinkedIn page for your company.
- [ ] **Product Demo**: A short video or link to a live demo of your product can be very effective.

By having all of these items prepared in advance, you can streamline the application process and present your startup in the most professional light possible.
